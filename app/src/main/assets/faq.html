• Why does it take a long time to sync and scan my library?

    If you have a large number of songs in your library, it may take a while to index them all so that we can send the song information to our servers and get mood tags. We have limited a single scan to 3,000 songs for better performance. If your library is larger, you can continue scanning it later until all your songs have been processed.

  • So what is music eScape all about?

    To look at the effects of music on changing your moods along a specific path.

  • What's the difference between playlist and a mood journey?

    A mood journey is a path that you create between moods on the draw screen. It contains a set of songs that are representative of the mood or series of moods you have drawn. A playlist always contains the same set of songs. If you like a particular playlist it’s a good idea to save it.

  • How do my songs get tagged with moods?

    Music eScape analyses your music’s energy and valence using The Echo Nest’s audio analysis data technology. It compares your songs to a large database and then assigns a mood tag to the song. If you disagree with the assigned tag from The Echo Nest then you can retag it to the mood you believe it should be.

  • On the draw screen why are some dots bigger than others?

    The size of the dots is relative to the intensity of the mood tag, the bigger the dot the more intense the mood.

  • Why are there sometimes gaps in my playlists?

    If there are no songs that are assigned to the specific area where you drew your journey then eScape bypasses that point.

  • Why can't I shuffle?

    Because you are creating a journey that has a distinct beginning middle and end according to the moods you choose. The good thing is you can take as many journeys as you like.

  • How do I go back to my mood journey/now playing screen?

    Press and hold down the player on the bottom of the screen.
